   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Emmett "Babe" Wallace (June 24, 1909 – December 3, 2006),^[1] was an
   American singer, stage performer, composer, actor, and poet. His decades
   long career included many years performing abroad in Europe and Israel. He
   featured on stage and screen. The New York Public Library has a collection
   of his papers in the Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture.^[2]

Biography

   [edit]

   Wallace was born in Brooklyn, New York on June 24, 1909, to George Bason
   and Inez Wallace.^[2] Prior to 1950, his given name was spelled "Emmett,"
   and after his name was spelled "Emett".^[1] He attended the Manual
   Training High School in Brooklyn but was unable to complete his
   education.^[2] He worked as busboy, messenger, and bouncer at the Savoy
   Ballroom in Harlem.^[2] Wallace had two children,^[2] a daughter from his
   marriage to Dorothy Hight, Carolyn Delores Wallace (born 1932 - 2001), and
   a son by Vivian Dandridge, Michael Emmett Wallace (born in 1943)

   In 1935, Wallace was part of the cast of, Cotton Club Parade alongside
   Lena Horne, which was performed at the Cotton Club.^[3] Wallace composed
   the song "A Chicken Ain't Nothin' but a Bird" recorded by Cab Calloway in
   1940.^[4] In 1945, he was to broadcast on the Gloom Dodgers show on radio
   station WHN.^[4] Wallace also appeared in Broadway's reproduction of the
   film classic "Guys and Dolls" and also contributed to the soundtrack to
   the play.

   The New York Public Library has a collection of his papers and photographs
   of Wallace.^[5]^[6] Carl Van Vechten photographed him and Yale University
   Library has the images in its archives.^[7] In 1940, Wallace was selected
   to front the Ella Fitzgerald Orchestra on tour.^[8] he died in 2006.

Filmography

   [edit]
     * The Black Network (1936)
     * The Devil's Daughter (1939) as John Lowden
     * Stormy Weather (1943) as Chick Bailey^[4]
     * Rhythm in a Riff (1946)
     * The Fight Never Ends (1948)^[9]

Compositions

   [edit]
     * "A Chicken Ain't Nothin' but a Bird"^[4]
     * "Get It, Get It, I Love to See You With It"^[4]
     * "That Feeling is Gone"^[4]
     * "America Forever"^[4]
     * "Bring Enough Clothes for Three Days",^[4] Timmie Rogers recorded it
       and it was a hit.^[10]
     * "Dorie Miller" about war hero Doris Miller^[11]
